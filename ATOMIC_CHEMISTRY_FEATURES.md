## üîó Chemical Bonding System

### Bond Types

1. **Single Bonds** (œÉ bonds)
   - Example: H-H in H‚ÇÇ
   - Shared electron pair
   - Bond energy: ~300-400 kJ/mol

2. **Double Bonds** (œÉ + œÄ)
   - Example: O=O in O‚ÇÇ
   - Two shared pairs
   - Bond energy: ~600-800 kJ/mol

3. **Triple Bonds** (œÉ + 2œÄ)
   - Example: N‚â°N in N‚ÇÇ
   - Three shared pairs
   - Bond energy: ~900+ kJ/mol
   - **WARNING: Often explosive!**

4. **Ionic Bonds**
   - Example: Na‚Å∫Cl‚Åª
   - Electron transfer
   - Forms when electronegativity difference > 1.7

### Bond Properties

- **Bond Energy** - Energy required to break (calculated realistically)
- **Bond Length** - Distance between nuclei (in angstroms)
- **Stability** - Checks if bond is chemically feasible

## üéì Educational Concepts Taught

### 1. Atomic Structure
- Protons define the element
- Electrons determine bonding
- Valence electrons are key to chemistry
- Electron shells follow the octet rule

### 2. Chemical Bonding
- Covalent vs. Ionic bonds
- Single, double, triple bonds
- Bond energy and stability
- Electronegativity and polarity

### 3. Molecular Stability
- **Stable**: All atoms have full valence shells
- **Metastable**: Can exist but may react
- **Unstable**: Will decompose or react quickly
- **Explosive**: Releases massive energy rapidly

### 4. Reaction Types
- **Synthesis**: A + B ‚Üí AB
- **Decomposition**: AB ‚Üí A + B
- **Combustion**: Fuel + O‚ÇÇ ‚Üí CO‚ÇÇ + H‚ÇÇO + energy
- **Neutralization**: Acid + Base ‚Üí Salt + Water

### 5. Chemical Safety
- Why some combinations are dangerous
- Oxidizers + Flammables = FIRE
- Unstable molecules can explode
- Proper handling and storage
